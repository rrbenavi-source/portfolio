Publication · Data Product Blueprint

The compass for building data products with real impact

Because success does not depend on technology, but on direction.

Author: Ricardo Benavides · Editing: Aleida Sepúlveda  ·  2025

The problem is not technical, it is about direction

Most data products fail not because of technical limits, but for lack of direction. They are born without a clear course: you invest in tools, you produce reports, you build models… and in the end, the business stays the same.

They operate without aligning to the business, and they attack symptoms, not real causes.

If the product does not generate impact, it is just digital decoration.

What do we want to change?

In the digital world it is easy to be dazzled by what is new and complex. But the real value is not in the tool, it is in how it is applied to generate impact, transform decisions and improve results.

Every project with impact starts with a clear, shared direction: why it matters to the business and how we will know we are making progress. When that clarity exists, strategy, technology and data stop competing and start working together.

A data product without purpose is like a GPS with no destination: it can be sophisticated, but it leads nowhere.

What the Data Product Blueprint is

Imagine a simple, visual, concrete roadmap that connects the business strategy with what your data team is building. That is the Data Product Blueprint: a practical framework that turns vision into action, purpose into metrics and ideas into results.

It combines the clarity of a board with Agile discipline, allowing a structured conversation to happen on a single page. It is not about ticking boxes, but about building a shared narrative that guides decisions and prioritizes what truly matters.

The questions many organizations avoid

  • What business pain are we solving?
  • What data do we really need?
  • Who will use the product, and how?
  • What happens if it fails?

The 10 pieces of the Blueprint

  1. 01

    Define the problem

    The pain or strategic need that drives the creation of the product.

  2. 02

    Adopt the solution

    Choose the best solution for the problem.

  3. 03

    Map the data

    Organize and structure the relevant data.

  4. 04

    Test hypotheses

    Verify the effectiveness of the hypotheses.

  5. 05

    Involve the actors

    Collaborate with customers and stakeholders.

  6. 06

    Execute strategies

    Implement strategic actions.

  7. 07

    Monitor KPIs

    Track key performance indicators.

  8. 08

    Assess values

    Determine the magnitude of the problem.

  9. 09

    Identify risks

    Recognize possible risks and challenges.

  10. 10

    Evaluate impact

    Measure the impact on the business.

Three domains: the 360° view

Strategy

The why and what for

Connects the product with the direction of the business and ensures every technical decision has a clear purpose.

Data & execution

The how

Structures the architecture, the flows and the rules that keep order, coherence and quality.

Impact

What we achieve

Evaluates results, measures risks and generates learnings that feed back into the continuous-improvement cycle.

What the Blueprint enables

  • Provide clarity on what to build, how to do it and why.
  • Coordinate diverse teams under a single story.
  • Avoid rework and disconnected decisions.
  • Measure progress not only by deliverables, but by real impact.

A compass, not a form

Data products are not a technical challenge, but a business decision. The Blueprint invites you to question before building: what problem do we want to solve?, what result do we expect to generate?, what decision will we be able to make thanks to this product?

It is not a form to fill in, it is a compass that guides. Strategy sets the direction, data and execution trace the path, and impact confirms we reached the right destination.

There is no point in building the perfect solution to the wrong problem. It is not about doing more things, but about doing the right thing.
