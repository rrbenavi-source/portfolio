From observation to prescriptive strategy

From descriptive visualization to prescriptive intelligence: the AI-driven analytics evolution.

Content: Ricardo Benavides · Editing: Aleida Sepúlveda  ·  2025

Why descriptive BI is no longer enough

For years, traditional Business Intelligence has revolved around static panels and descriptive reports. Useful, yes, but deeply reactive.

Today, leaders do not only want to know what happened: they need to know what is happening now, why it is happening and what the recommended action is.

A typical consumer-goods case

Every week, management reviewed between 10 and 15 reports, spending the session validating data instead of deciding. When a stockout happened, the datasets had shown the signal a week earlier, but the connection of the insight was lost among isolated reports.

The pattern is clear: descriptive BI presents information, but it neither prioritizes it nor explains causes.

Prescriptive intelligence: activating insights with AI

According to Gartner, this evolution is known as Augmented Analytics, where AI automates the discovery of insights and reduces the dependence on manual, technical analysis.

What turns data into decisions

  1. 01

    Automated insights

    Dashboards no longer just show charts: they generate ready-to-use conclusions, such as “your fill rate dropped 3 points in the North region, associated with this distributor and these SKUs”.

  2. 02

    Alerts and causality

    AI identifies deviations and sends proactive alerts immediately, explaining in natural language what caused the change.

  3. 03

    Causality insights

    Generates executive analyses that identify the nature of the variation and its root causes.

  4. 04

    Predictive models

    Through machine learning, it anticipates demand peaks, scrap, low turnover or stockout risks, enabling action before operational impact.

Prescriptive intelligence interprets signals, detects causes and recommends actions before problems occur.

How to start the transition without disruption

  • Alerts, especially on deviations.
  • KPIs: fill rate, OTIF, scrap, OEE, contribution margin.
  • Forecasting of demand, turnover or operational risk.

Case study: consumer-goods industry

In a typical operation with SAP, a corporate data warehouse and Power BI/Tableau, a Prescriptive Intelligence model was implemented to monitor fill rate by region, channel and SKU; detect deterioration patterns related to inventory, orders and production; and issue automated alerts when conditions linked to stockouts recurred.

Business-case results (6–12 months of maturation)

  • A visible decrease in stockouts.
  • More efficient management meetings.
  • Dashboards that become action tools, not just visualization.
If your BI only documents the past, it is time to evolve toward systems that guide the future.
